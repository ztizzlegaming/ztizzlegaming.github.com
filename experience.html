August 2018 - May 2019

Math and Computer Science Research Position, Centre College

I had the opportunity to conduct research with Dr. Michael Lamar and another student at Centre College. During this research project, we developed and implemented a statistical learning model applied to the Netflix Prize problem. We applied the Co-Occurance Data Embedding (CODE) algorithm to the Likert-scale data of the Netflix Prize. We analyzed the Netflix Prize dataset, which consisted of over 100 million data points, and used our own custom built program in C++ which we ran on a powerful Linux machine. We learn an embedding of the users and movies, and use this embedding to make a prediction on a user's rating of a movie. The algorithm is fast, simple, and intuitive, and the predictions it makes match those of other more complex approaches. We are publishing a paper and presented our findings at the Joint Math Meetings in Baltimore.

View the project here: https://github.com/ztizzlegaming/netflix-prize
